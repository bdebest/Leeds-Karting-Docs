1. Introduction

This handbook outlines the expected behavior and rules for all members of the Leeds Karting community, including players and staff. It also details the consequences for violating these guidelines to ensure a fair, respectful, and enjoyable environment for everyone. Adherence to these guidelines is crucial for maintaining the integrity and positive atmosphere of Leeds Karting.

2. General Conduct Guidelines

All interactions within the Leeds Karting community, whether in-game, on Discord, or other official platforms, must be respectful, constructive, and professional.

  • Respectful Communication: Treat all individuals with respect, regardless of their role, skill level, or background. Avoid any form of harassment, bullying, hate speech, discrimination, or offensive language.
  • Privacy: Do not share personal information of others without their explicit consent. Respect the privacy of all community members and staff.
  • Roblox Terms of Service: All users and staff must strictly adhere to the Roblox Terms of Service and Community Guidelines. Any violation of Roblox's rules will also be considered a violation of Leeds Karting's guidelines.
  • Positive Environment: Contribute positively to the community. Engage in constructive discussions and help foster a welcoming atmosphere for new and existing members.
  • Account Security: Be responsible for your account security. Do not share your login credentials with anyone.

3. In-Game Rules & Fair Play

To ensure fair competition and a positive racing experience for everyone, the following in-game rules must be strictly observed:

  • No Exploiting or Cheating: The use of any unauthorized third-party software, scripts, or glitches to gain an unfair advantage (e.g., speed hacks, teleporting, bypassing game mechanics) is strictly forbidden and will result in severe consequences.
  • Fair Racing Conduct: Intentional crashing, ramming, blocking, or griefing other players is prohibited. Maintain fair racing lines and allow for respectful overtakes.
  • Staying on Track: Racers must remain within the designated track boundaries at all times. Cutting corners or driving off-track to gain an advantage is not permitted.
  • Respecting Staff Instructions: Players must immediately comply with instructions given by Track Marshals, General Managers, or any other authorized Leeds Karting staff member during sessions.
  • Appropriate In-Game Chat: Keep in-game chat respectful and relevant to the game. Avoid spamming, excessive caps, or any form of disruptive communication.
  • No Impersonation: Do not impersonate other players, staff members, or any official Leeds Karting entity.

4. Staff Responsibilities in Upholding Guidelines

Leeds Karting staff members are held to the highest standards of conduct and are crucial in maintaining a positive and fair environment. Your role involves both adhering to and enforcing these guidelines.

  • Role Models: Staff members are expected to be role models for the community, demonstrating exemplary behavior in all interactions, both in-game and on official platforms.
  • Fair Enforcement: Enforce all guidelines fairly, consistently, and without bias. Personal feelings should never interfere with the application of rules.
  • Knowledge of Rules: All staff must have a thorough understanding of this handbook and all other Leeds Karting policies to effectively guide and correct community members.
  • Professionalism in Enforcement: When addressing violations, maintain a calm, professional, and objective demeanor. Avoid engaging in arguments or retaliatory behavior.
  • Upholding the Code of Conduct: Staff members are bound by the same Code of Conduct as all community members, with additional responsibilities related to their positions. Any violation by staff will be subject to disciplinary action, potentially more severe due to the breach of trust and authority.

5. Reporting Violations (Staff & Community)

Maintaining a safe and fair environment is a collective effort. We encourage both staff and community members to report violations of these guidelines.

5.1 Reporting by Community Members

  • Players should use the designated in-game reporting tools or create a support ticket via our official Discord server or website if available.
  • Provide clear evidence (screenshots, video recordings, chat logs) when reporting.

5.2 Reporting by Staff Members

Staff members have a duty to report violations they witness or are made aware of.

  • Reporting Players: Follow the established ticket system or internal reporting procedures for player violations. Provide detailed accounts and evidence.
  • Reporting Staff Members: If a staff member observes another staff member violating the Code of Conduct or any company policy, they are obligated to report it. This must be done respectfully and professionally, following the Chain of Command:
    1. Report to your immediate Supervisor.
    2. If the Supervisor is involved or unresponsive, report to a General Manager.
    3. For severe issues involving General Managers or if other channels are exhausted, report directly to a member of the Board of Directors.
  • Confidentiality: All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality to protect all parties involved. Retaliation against individuals who report violations in good faith is strictly prohibited.

6. Disciplinary Actions & Process

Violations of these guidelines will result in disciplinary action. The specific actions taken will depend on the severity, frequency, and nature of the offense. For a detailed overview of common offenses and their corresponding consequences, please refer to the **Guidelines & Consequences Table** document.

Offense Type Severity First Offense Subsequent Offenses
Minor Misconduct (e.g., minor chat spam) Low Verbal Warning Temporary Mute (15 min)
Moderate Misconduct (e.g., mild disrespect) Medium Official Warning Temporary Ban (1-24 hours)
Major Misconduct (e.g., exploiting, severe harassment) High Permanent Ban Permanent Ban
