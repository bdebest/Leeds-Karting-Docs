1. Welcome to the Team!

This handbook serves as your comprehensive guide to being a valuable and integral member of the Leeds Karting staff. It outlines our expectations, policies, and the resources available to you, ensuring a clear understanding of your role within our dynamic Roblox go-karting community.

We are committed to fostering a positive, productive, and respectful environment where every team member can thrive, contribute their best, and help us deliver an unparalleled experience to our racers. Your dedication and professionalism are key to our collective success.

2. Roles & Responsibilities

Leeds Karting operates with a dedicated team across various departments, each crucial to our overall success. Below are the departments and the general roles within them.

2.1 Departments & Their Functions

  • Guest Services: Responsible for racer bookings, organizing schedules, conducting briefings, and ensuring a smooth customer journey from arrival to departure.
  • First Aid: Provides realistic and timely first aid care to racers and staff in case of any incidents on or off the track.
  • Track Marshall: Ensures track safety, clears the track of any hazards, assists with race management, and helps run sessions efficiently.
  • Developer: Focuses on the continuous development and improvement of the Leeds Karting game, including new features, bug fixes, and performance optimization.
  • Kart Mechanic: Maintains the go-karts, performs regular checks, conducts repairs, and ensures all karts are fully functioning and safe for use.
  • Security: Manages crowd control, helps de-escalate situations with agitated racers or community members, and ensures all staff and members adhere to respectful conduct guidelines.
  • General Manager: Oversees daily operations, hosts sessions, manages staff across departments, and handles escalated complaints.

2.2 Role Tiers (Applicable to all departments except General Manager)

  • Supervisor: Responsible for overseeing staff within their respective department, providing direct guidance, and ensuring departmental goals are met.
  • Senior Employee: An experienced team member who assists staff within their department, provides training to junior employees, and acts as a point of contact for complex tasks.
  • Standard Employee: Performs the core duties and responsibilities assigned to their specific department.
  • Employee in Training: A new employee who is currently undergoing the necessary training to become a Standard Employee.
  • Employee under Investigation: An employee who is being investigated for a potential breach of company policy or conduct. Their status and duties are pending the outcome of the investigation and potential punishment.
  • Suspended Employee: An employee who is temporarily prohibited from performing their job duties, awaiting further review, disciplinary action, or the conclusion of an investigation.

3. Chain of Command

The following chain of command must be respected and followed at all times to ensure clear communication, efficient decision-making, and proper escalation of issues. Always report to your direct superior unless explicitly instructed otherwise or in an emergency situation requiring immediate higher-level intervention.

  2. General Managers
  3. Supervisors

  5. Standard Employee

4. Code of Conduct

All members of the Leeds Karting team are expected to adhere to a high standard of conduct. This includes, but is not limited to: treating all colleagues and players with respect and professionalism, maintaining a positive and constructive attitude, adhering to all Roblox terms of service and community guidelines, avoiding any form of harassment, discrimination, or bullying, and representing Leeds Karting positively both on and off the platform.

5. Safety Procedures

Ensuring the safety of our staff and racers is paramount at Leeds Karting. All personnel must strictly adhere to the following safety procedures:

  • Track Safety Checks: Track Marshals must conduct pre-session and post-incident checks to ensure the track is clear of debris, karts, or any hazards.
  • Kart Maintenance: Kart Mechanics are responsible for daily inspections and regular maintenance of all karts to ensure they are in optimal and safe working condition. Any kart deemed unsafe must be immediately taken out of service.
  • Emergency Response: All staff, especially First Aid Officers and Track Marshals, must be familiar with emergency protocols, including how to respond to in-game incidents, player distress, or technical malfunctions that could pose a risk. Clear communication channels for emergencies must be utilized.
  • Briefing & Instructions: Guest Services Associates and Track Marshals must provide clear and concise safety briefings to all racers before sessions, covering track rules, kart operation, and emergency procedures.
  • Incident Reporting: Any safety incident, no matter how minor, must be reported immediately to a Supervisor or General Manager. Detailed incident reports are crucial for continuous improvement of our safety measures.
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): While in a virtual environment, staff are expected to role-play and encourage the use of appropriate virtual safety gear for racers where applicable, promoting a culture of safety.

Adherence to these procedures is non-negotiable and contributes directly to the safe and enjoyable experience for everyone at Leeds Karting.

6. Communication Guidelines

Effective communication is the backbone of a successful team and a thriving community. All Leeds Karting staff must adhere to the following guidelines:

6.1 Internal Communication (Staff to Staff)

  • Primary Channels: Utilize designated Discord channels for general announcements, departmental discussions, and direct messaging for urgent matters. Avoid unnecessary private messages for issues that concern the wider team.
  • Professionalism: Maintain a professional and respectful tone in all communications, even in informal settings. Avoid slang, excessive emojis, or language that could be misinterpreted.
  • Reporting Issues: Follow the Chain of Command when reporting issues or seeking approvals. Direct concerns to your immediate Supervisor first.
  • Feedback: Provide constructive feedback to colleagues and supervisors. Be open to receiving feedback as well, as it is vital for personal and team growth.
  • Punctuality in Responses: Respond to internal messages and requests in a timely manner, especially for urgent operational matters.

6.2 External Communication (Staff to Racers/Community)

  • Official Tone: When interacting with racers or the wider community (in-game chat, public Discord channels, social media), represent Leeds Karting with a helpful, friendly, and professional demeanor.
  • Clarity & Accuracy: Provide clear, accurate, and consistent information regarding rules, events, and game updates. If unsure, consult a Supervisor before responding.
  • Handling Complaints: Listen actively to complaints or concerns. Attempt to resolve minor issues politely. For escalated or complex complaints, direct racers to the appropriate support channels (e.g., ticket system) or escalate to a General Manager.
  • Confidentiality: Never share internal discussions, personal staff information, or unreleased game details with the public.
  • Brand Representation: Remember that your actions and words reflect on Leeds Karting. Always strive to uphold our positive brand image.

By adhering to these communication guidelines, we ensure a harmonious working environment and a positive experience for our entire community.
